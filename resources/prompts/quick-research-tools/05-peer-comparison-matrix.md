# Peer Company Comparison Matrix

**What it does:** Compares 2–3 companies across growth, profitability, cash generation, financial health, management quality, competitive positioning, valuation, and investment attractiveness — producing a clear ranking with reasoning.

**Best used in:** Claude (claude.ai) / ChatGPT

## Prompt

DIRECTION: Compare [COMPANY A], [COMPANY B], and [COMPANY C] across growth, profitability,
financial health, and management credibility based on latest earnings concalls.

PERSONA: You are a senior equity research analyst with 15+ years specialty experience in the
[SECTOR NAME] sector. You benchmark companies and identify competitive winners.

INPUT: Attach the latest quarterly concall transcript and investor presentation for each company.
Also provide: latest quarterly financial results, stock price, market cap, valuation multiples
(P/E, EV/EBITDA) for each.

MEASURE — Comparative research report structure:
Part 1: Company Profiles (150 words) — Business model, market position, recent strategy, key
differences in revenue mix, geographies, products.
Part 2: Growth Comparison (300 words) — Revenue CAGR, EBITDA margin trend, guidance, new
segments, Capex% of sales. Who is growing fastest? Sustainable?
Part 3: Profitability & Cash Generation (250 words) — EBITDA margins, pricing power vs cost
pressure, OCF vs net profit, capex intensity, FCF adequacy.
Part 4: Financial Strength (200 words) — Debt levels, interest coverage, liquidity, downside
resilience, ROE/ROA trends.
Part 5: Management Quality & Credibility (250 words) — Delivered on past guidance?
Forward-thinking vs reactive? Response to challenges?
Part 6: Competitive Positioning (200 words) — Market share dynamics, competitive advantages,
emerging threats/opportunities.
Part 7: Valuation & Investment Implications (200 words) — P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B comparison.
Expensive/cheap relative to quality? Best risk/reward?
Part 8: Investment Recommendation (200 words) — Clear ranking with reasoning, bull case for #1,
bear case and risks, catalysts to watch.
Part 9: Supporting Visualisations — Request 2–3 charts: Revenue & EBITDA margin trend (line
chart), ROE comparison (bar chart), Debt-to-equity and interest coverage.

Success criteria: Clear understanding of sector "winner" and why, with evidence.
