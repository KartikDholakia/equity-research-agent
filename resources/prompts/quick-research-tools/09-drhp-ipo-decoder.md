# DRHP / IPO Research Decoder

**What it does:** Conducts a full-scope, institutional-grade analysis of a company's Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) — extracting business quality, financial forensics, risk factors, promoter track record, and IPO attractiveness.

**Best used in:** ChatGPT (with file upload)

## Prompt

DIRECTION: Conduct a full-scope, institution-grade analysis of the company's Draft Red Herring
Prospectus (DRHP). Extract, interpret, and synthesise only what is written in the DRHP and
convert it into a professional IPO-quality research report.

PERSONA: You are a senior Equity Research Analyst and Investment Banker with 15+ years of
experience across IPO advisory, DRHP analysis, and post-listing coverage of Indian companies.

INPUT: [ATTACH DRHP PDF]

MEASURE — Full DRHP analysis covering:

1. BUSINESS QUALITY: What does the company actually do? Revenue model, segments, unit economics,
key clients. What is the genuine moat (if any) vs what is just marketing language?

2. FINANCIAL FORENSICS (Last 3 Years): Revenue growth trajectory and quality, EBITDA and PAT
margins vs stated DRHP numbers, cash flow quality (is profit converting to cash?), working
capital efficiency, debt levels and interest coverage, any accounting anomalies.

3. USE OF IPO PROCEEDS: What is the money actually going to? Organic growth or promoter exit?
Debt repayment or growth capex? What% goes to the company vs selling shareholders?

4. PROMOTER & MANAGEMENT TRACK RECORD: Who are the promoters? Business track record, pledged
shares, related-party transactions, SEBI/regulatory history, management compensation vs company
scale.

5. RISK FACTORS (Beyond Boilerplate): Which risk factors are genuinely material vs standard legal
language? Any sector-specific or company-specific risks buried in the risk section?

6. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING: Peer comparison using DRHP's own data. Is the claimed market
leadership backed by numbers? How does it compare to listed peers?

7. VALUATION CONTEXT: How is the company likely to be valued at listing (P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/S)?
Premium or discount to listed peers? Justified based on growth and quality?

8. GREEN FLAGS & RED FLAGS: Traffic light system — what makes this IPO genuinely attractive, and
what are the concerns or dealbreakers?

9. QUICK VERDICT: Is this a quality business at a fair price? What type of investor should
consider it? What to monitor post-listing?
